UNDERSTANDING FORM 16 AND FORM 16A

Now it is a season to get Form 16 and Form 16A form different sources. These are required to file tax return. In simple words, these are proofs for your income and tax paid to the government. So it becomes very important to have these forms for filing income tax returns.

What is form 16?

It is a certificate issued by employer to employee giving the details of salary earned during the period and tax deducted at source on behalf and paid to government.

It has 3 parts. Part A downloaded from the Income Tax website containing quarter wise details of tax deducted and remitted; Part B containing details of salary paid, exemptions considered, deductions allowed, tax computed and tax deducted; Part C Form 12 BA giving breakup of perquisites, if any.

Important points to remember about form 16

  1. It is a certificate of deduction of tax at source issued under section 203 of the Income Tax Act, 1961.
  2. IT is the final certificate issued by your employer
  3. It is given at the end of the financial year, due date of issuing Form 16 is May 31st
  4. If there has been no TDS on the salary, it is not compulsory to issue Form 16 by the employer. Instead you will get salary certificate.
  5. Receiving form 16 is different from filing tax returns.
  6. Form 16 has details of the date of tax deduction and the branch of the bank where it is deposited into the central government account.
  7. If TDS is deducted by banks to the pension holder, banks also need to issue Form 16.

What if you have changed jobs during the financial year?

You need to collect Form 16 from both previous and current employer at the end of the year. When you join a new organisation, you should furnish TDS details from the previous employer to current employer. This will help current employer in deducting tax accordingly.

Do you have to attach form 16 with IT returns?

As per the IT department, it is not necessary to attach the original form 16 with your income-tax returns.

What are the important details to be checked in FORM 16?

  1. Heading – Certificate under Section 203 of the Income Tax Act, 1961 for tax deducted at source from income chargeable under the head “Salaries”
  2. PAN number: check whether your PAN number is taken correctly (particularly in PART A). If it is wrong, ask your employer to rectify it and give you a new Form 16.
  3. Your employer’s name and address
  4. Your employer’s Permanent Account Number (PAN) and Tax Deduction Account Number (TAN)
  5. Your name and designation
  6. Which year the Form 16 belong to e.g. Period 01/04/2011 to 31/03/2012

What if there is an error in figures in Form 16?

If there is an error by the employer, you could request them to rectify it and issue a revised Form 16. If a higher tax has been deducted, you can claim tax refund while filing your returns.

There may be chances of mismatch of figures as per Form 16 with the tax declaration statement provided by you to organization at the beginning of the year. Possible reasons could be

  • Figures mentioned are either wrong or not considered at all. The result would be that fewer deductions have been made, resulting in higher tax liability
  • You might not have submitted the proofs of all investments. Result again would be higher deduction of tax.

Lost Form 16? What is the remedy?

  • Request your employer to issue a copy of Form 16 for the past year
  • If they do not oblige, request them to issue a certificate stating your gross earnings and tax deducted at source for that particular period.
  • Download the tax deducted details from Form 26AS

What is Form 16A?

The purpose of form 16 and Form 16A is same except issuing person is other than employer. In other words it is also a certificate of income and tax deducted at source with the details of tax paid to government on your behalf other than salary income.

Following are examples of circumstances for which you get Form 16A

  1. For interest on securities e.g.; FD interest paid by Banks
  2. Interest other than interest on securities
  3. Payments to contractors and sub- contractors
  4. Insurance commission received by insurance agents
  5. Rent received
  6. Fees for professional or technical services
  7. Consultancy income received
